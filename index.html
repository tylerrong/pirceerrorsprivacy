What is a price error?
A price error is when a retailer accidentally lists a product far below its intended price — a $249 gadget showing up at $24.99, a stacking coupon that shouldn't work, or an item ringing up free. They're caused by typos, feed glitches, and automation mistakes, and they usually get fixed within minutes to hours. Read the full guide.
Is it legal to buy a price error?
Yes. Buying a product at the price a retailer lists is completely legal. The retailer may cancel the order before it ships if they catch the mistake, but there's nothing illegal about checking out at the listed price.
Do retailers actually honor price mistakes?
Often, yes. Many orders ship before the error is noticed, and some retailers honor the price to avoid disappointing customers. Speed matters — the faster you check out after an alert, the better your odds.
Is the Price Errors app free?

How fast are the alerts?
